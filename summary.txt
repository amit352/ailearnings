Why Google shows “Alternate page with canonical tag”

This happens when multiple URLs lead to the same content and Google chooses one main version to index.

Typical cases for your site:

1️⃣ Root vs section navigation

Example URLs Google may see:

https://ailearnings.in/
https://ailearnings.in/#roadmap
https://ailearnings.in/#phase-1

These are same page with anchors, so Google indexes only the root page.

2️⃣ Canonical pointing to homepage

If your site has something like:

<link rel="canonical" href="https://ailearnings.in/">

then pages like:

/resources
/readiness
/prep-plan

may be treated as alternate versions.

But your pages actually exist:

/resources

/readiness

/prep-plan

/assessment

/genai-guide

These should ideally canonicalize to themselves, not homepage.

What you should check (important)

Open any page source and confirm canonical tag.

Example for this page:

https://ailearnings.in/resources/

Correct canonical should be:

<link rel="canonical" href="https://ailearnings.in/resources/" />

NOT

<link rel="canonical" href="https://ailearnings.in/" />
